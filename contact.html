What is microblading?

Microblading is a semi-permanent form of makeup using a hand tool comprised of 11-26 blades in the hands of a precise artist. The byproduct of microblading is natural looking eyebrows that you wonâ€™t have to fill in every day. Microblading is a two-step process, the initial procedure and a touch up which occurs ~4-6 weeks after.


Does microblading hurt?

What the average person feels during a microblading procedure is similar to plucking of the eyebrow. The technician will apply a primary and a secondary numbing cream throughout the procedure to ensure the client is numb to prevent any pain.


How long does a microblading appointment last?

Each appointment will take anywhere from 1.5-2.5 hours depending on client skin type and type of microblading. The touch up takes about 1 hour.


Is microblading for every brow type?

Yes! Microblading is suitable for everybody. Whether your eyebrows are light and sparce or dark and bushy.


How does skin type affect microblading?

Skin type has an impact on the effectiveness of microblading. For example, people with dry skin are able to hold pigment better and longer. On the other hand, those with oily skin may require more frequent touch ups due to the pigment not holding as well.


What do I need to know before getting microbladed?

In order to have the best microblading experience, please follow these pre-care instructions! Doing so will assure better retention and easy healing. Please come with a fresh face and no make up on your eyebrows.
  • No tanning, facials, or botox two weeks prior to procedure

  • No blood thinners within 3 days of appointment (ex: Aspirin, Ibuprofen, Vitamin E, Fish Oil)

  • No waxing or tinting eyebrows 3 days prior to procedure

  • Do not consume alcohol 24-48 hours before procedure

  • Do not consumer coffee before procedure

  • Must be off Accutane for at least a year
